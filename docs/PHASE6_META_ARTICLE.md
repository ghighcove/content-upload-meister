### Introduction (Hook)
- Open with the core paradox: optimizing for Google HURTS LLM discoverability
- Present the specific conflict: "10 Amazing Tips!" (SEO: 85, GEO: 45) vs.
  "Quantitative Framework for..." (SEO: 40, GEO: 90)
- Thesis: The optimization space is 3D, not 1D. Most content creators only see one axis.

### Part 1: The Three Axes (Technical)
- Axis 1: Traditional SEO — what Google wants
- Axis 2: GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — what LLMs want
- Axis 3: Platform Constraints — what Medium/Substack actually allow
- Bonus: Voice Preservation — what your readers came for
- **Show**: The actual conflict matrix with real examples from our data

### Part 2: The Optimization Engine
- How we modeled this as a multi-objective optimization problem
- The 5 strategies and when to use each
- What the Pareto front reveals (the "honest trade-off curve")
- **Show**: The 3D interactive visualization (embed or screenshot)

### Part 3: The Second Dimension Set (The Bigger Picture)
- Introduce the content-style testing matrix (from second opinion doc)
- Tone × Depth × Data Presentation as a SEPARATE 3D space
- The real question: how do these two 3D spaces INTERACT?
- Hypothesis: Technical tone naturally scores higher on GEO; accessible scores higher on traditional SEO
- **This is where it gets genuinely novel**: a 6D optimization problem

### Part 4: What We Found (Test Results)
- Real data from our 30-case matrix (Phase 4 results)
- Platform-specific findings (Substack vs. Medium)
- Surprising result: "platform_first" strategy consistently wins combined score
  → Implication: meeting constraints IS the optimization, not fighting them
- The voice-preservation penalty: what authenticity costs in discoverability

### Part 5: The Human-AI Collaboration Layer
- What I can't automate (yet): strategic decisions about which axis matters most
- What AI handles well: constraint satisfaction within a defined strategy
- The meta-insight: AI generates the optimization space, human chooses the path
- Connection to the broader trend: AI as co-author vs. AI as optimizer vs. AI as publisher

### Part 6: What This Means for Content Creators
- Practical takeaways: pick your primary axis before optimizing
- Platform selection as SEO decision (Substack = GEO, Medium = traditional SEO)
- The 1:4-8 rule (1 Medium article per 4-8 Substack) as emergent optimization strategy
- Building toward: recommendation engine based on content metadata

### Conclusion: The Paradox Resolved
- You can't perfectly optimize all three dimensions simultaneously
- But you CAN choose the right trade-off for your goals
- The optimization space is a tool for making that choice visible
- "The AI doesn't resolve the paradox — it makes the trade-offs legible"
